About

I am a student at Harvard University studying mathematics and computer science, as well as a competitive programmer and musician.

I love programming and am usually working on side projects. These cover a wide range of topics, including machine learning, programming languages, systems, computer graphics, and music.

My academic interests are in algorithms, machine learning, and predictive modeling. I am currently a researcher in the Predictive Medicine Group at Harvard Medical School. Also, I was a teaching fellow for CS 124 (Data Structures and Algorithms) in Spring 2020, with Michael Mitzenmacher.

Competitive Programming

In high school, I won two gold medals representing the US at the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI), ranking 7th globally. I have helped organize or write problems for several major programming competitions, including:

  • USA Computing Olympiad (USACO)
  • National Olympiad in Informatics Philippines (NOI.PH)
  • PWSH Programming Contest
  • HackerEarth CodeStar Contest

In addition, I maintain an advanced algorithm and data structure library available here. If you are looking for tutoring, I teach online and summer camp courses with Alphastar Academy for motivated students aiming to perform at a high level in the USACO contests.
