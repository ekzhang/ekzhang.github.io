About

I am a student at Harvard University studying computer science and mathematics, as well as a competitive programmer and musician.

I love thinking about programming languages, graphics, machine learning, and systems. I also build projects!
  • Rpt, a path tracer (photorealistic rendering engine) from scratch.
  • Crepe, a compiled Datalog engine in Rust used by security researchers.
  • FastSeg, real-time semantic segmentation models for autonomous vehicles.
  • Set with Friends, a popular web application for playing Set online (400k+ users).

I've previously worked at Scale AI, improving infrastructure tools for a world-class ML team; at Nvidia, researching state-of-the-art deep learning research models for computer vision tasks; and at Harvard Medical School, using data to help physicians identify critical patients at risk of suicide.

At Harvard, I have been a teaching fellow for upper-level classes like CS 124, CS 161, and CS 182. I am also involved with chamber music and theatre productions, and I was principal viola of the Harvard-Radcliffe Orchestra.

Competitive Programming

In high school, I won two gold medals representing the US at the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI), placing 7th globally. I also ranked highly in the national math and physics olympiads, and I compete on Harvard's ICPC team.

I have written and tested problems for major national and international programming competitions, including the following:

  • USA Computing Olympiad (USACO)
  • National Olympiad in Informatics Philippines (NOI.PH)
  • PWSH Programming Contest
  • HackerEarth CodeStar Contest

My advanced algorithm and data structure library is available here. If you are looking for tutoring, I have previously taught recorded courses with Alphastar Academy for motivated students aiming to perform at a high level in programming competitions.
